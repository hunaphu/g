s=>s.replace(/\)\d?.|\(\w{0,3}./g,s=>s[2]?String.fromCodePoint(parseInt(s.slice(1),36)):s[1].toUpperCase())

Method

Our regular expression is made of two main parts:

  • \)\d?. matches )), )", )a and )xx
  • \(\w{0,3}. matches (( and (xxxx

where a is a lower case letter and xx / xxxx are base-36 strings.

NB: Nothing else will be match provided that the input string does not contain invalid tokens, as specified in the challenge.

Once we have a match s, we don't care about the escape character anymore and it boils down to two cases:

  • If s is at least 3 characters long, proceed with base-36 decoding which, alas, is quite verbose in JS:

    String.fromCodePoint(parseInt(s.slice(1),36))

  • Otherwise, return the 2nd character of s in upper case (leaving (, ) and " unchanged):

    s[1].toUpperCase()
