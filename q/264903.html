s/^(.*)(.).* \1(.).*/\2\30123456789/;s/^(.)(.).*\1.*\2.*/</;s/^(.)(.).*\2.*\1.*/>/;s/...*/=/

Assuming, that both numbers are in one line, space separated. Unfortunately, sed doesn't know how compare numbers, so we need to add the digit list and do some regexp backreferencing stuff. Option -E used for extended regular expressions to avoid twelve more bytes for escaping:

  • ^(.*)(.).* \1(.).*/\2\30123456789/ removes everything but the first differing digit and adds the ordered digits, so 42 43000 would turn into 230123456789
  • s/^(.)(.).*\1.*\2.*/</ turn the whole line into <, if the first digit appears in the digit list before the second one
  • s/^(.)(.).*\2.*\1.*/>/ does the opposite with >
  • s/...*/=/ cares for the rest (all legal lines that have not been replaced by < or > contain at least two characters (..* is one byte shorter than .{2,})
