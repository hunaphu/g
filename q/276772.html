=let(x,"Hello, World!",y,"Hello, World!",if(x=y,x,"Hello, World!"))

The formula can take a hit in any of the three Hello Worlds. The only way to bitflip that string so that it throws an error would be to change one of the letters to ", and that cannot happen because the string doesn't contain a b.

The formula can also take many kinds of hits in the middle x and the latter y, and certain kinds of hits elsewhere, but I'm those "safe" hits for the time being.

Hits in these substrings will thus be assumed to be fatal:
